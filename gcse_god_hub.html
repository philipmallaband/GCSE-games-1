⚡ Quick Revision — Key Terms

TeleologicalRelated to purpose and design — the Design Argument is the teleological argument
Paley's WatchThe analogy: finding a watch implies a watchmaker; the universe implies God
Infinite RegressA chain of causes going back forever — Aquinas said this is impossible
Necessary BeingA being that must exist (God) — unlike contingent beings that might not
Moral EvilEvil caused by human free choices (murder, war, injustice)
Natural EvilEvil caused by nature (earthquakes, disease, floods)
Augustine's TheodicyEvil came through the Fall — human free will broke a perfect world
Irenaeus's TheodicySoul-making: evil helps humans develop morally and spiritually
OmnipotentAll-powerful — one of God's traditional attributes
OmnibenevolentAll-good/all-loving — another traditional attribute of God
Hume's CriticismWe can't compare the universe to machines — we've only seen one universe
Free Will DefenceGenuine freedom requires the possibility of evil (Plantinga)
